Buttons

Buttons signal actions.

Accessibility

Graphic contrast should be at a ratio of at least 3:1.

Search button

A search button may be represented in one of two ways. It may feature the text "Search" or an icon.

Search icon
search
<i class="material-icons">search</i>
Search text
<button type="button" class="btn btn-primary">Search</button>

Pay now buttons

If the user has the ability to pay on their account online, a button allowing users to access ePay will be included in Financials in the balance card components.

Veering from the general rules surrounding headings and button text, for this button, the text will be in all caps to signify its importance.

<button type="button" class="btn btn-warning">PAY NOW</button>

Usability

When to use

Use buttons for the most important actions on the page.

Guidance
  • Use sentence case for button labels
  • Make the first word on buttons a verb
  • Consider adding an icon to indicate specific actions
  • Make sure buttons look clickable - use color variations to distinguish static, hover and active states
